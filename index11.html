METAGENESIS
Project Room
developing through the
subversion of biology

The creation of a narrative that subverts the boundaries between fact and fiction implied a process materialised through an editorial object to archive all the information behind the development of Metagenesis. The Codex is divided into four installments.

The codex

◄ ►
The First Installment, A New Foothold—The Set of a New Entity , deals with the setting of a fiction based on Julia Ducournau’s Titane. We have departed from the idea of identity as a notion that regulates the categorizations attributed to human beings according to stabilizing and fixed concepts of sex, gender and sexuality. The existing perception of this set of concepts is challenged by those who deviate from the standards of conformity, whose gender is incoherent or discontinuous. The concept of identity is questioned as well as what conventionally consolidates the assumption that it is uniform, constant over time, and standardised.

The Second Installment, Emerging—Beyond Gender, Sexuality, and Humanity, explores the concepts introduced previously, applying them in the proposed fiction—a society where the concept of identity is questioned through the emergence of a new being, whose conception develops through the subversion of biological processes. This instalment also sheds some light on what led us to wove this intricate fabric Metagenesis sits on. There is an exposition of the three phases of this being, which will cover the moment of biological creation—Conception—birth—Genesis—and, finally, development—Growth.

The Third Installment, Transposing—Essence Transforming Into Existence, widens the process responsible for the materialisation of the Project Room, an immersive space that provoked a multisensory experience that gave form to the fiction itself, that gave voice and purpose to a newborn that deviates gender norms, and dilates the next evolutionary step. The ambience had multiple layers that shaped the narrative, the light fluctuations, the sound, the visceral and enigmatic images—all devising an intrauterine environment to leave the spectator in an ever-lasting suspense.

The Fourth Installment, The Miracle of Life—A New Evolution, casts the nuances of an epilogue that does not propose to conclude the fiction, but instead justifies and perpetuates the suspense desired by both the narrative and its materialisation. On a personal note, we not only disclaim how we perceived the whole process and its pertinence by challenging a set of human constructs, but we also speculate about the fiction’s suspension of disbelief as well as its next step. Just as any biological evolutionary process, we state how our fiction descended from an original single cell and branched out almost infinitely.
